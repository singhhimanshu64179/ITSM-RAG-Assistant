Title: Major Incident Management SOP

Definition:
A Major Incident (MI) is a high-impact, high-urgency incident requiring immediate coordinated response.

Criteria:
- Critical service outage affecting entire business function
- Multiple sites or users impacted
- Severe financial impact
- Customer-facing downtime exceeding SLA threshold

Process:
1. Detection:
   - From monitoring or Service Desk.

2. Validation:
   - L1 checks impact and urgency.
   - If meets MI criteria â†’ notify Duty Manager.

3. MI Declaration:
   - Major Incident Manager (MIM) declares MI.
   - Bridge call initiated within 15 minutes.

4. Communication:
   - Stakeholders notified via email or Teams bridge.
   - Updates every 30 minutes.
   - Incident banner on dashboard.

5. Resolution:
   - Technical teams focus on restoring service quickly.
   - Workaround accepted temporarily.

6. Post-Incident Review:
   - PIR within 5 business days.
   - Identify RCA and corrective actions.

Roles:
- MIM: Owns incident end-to-end.
- Resolver Groups: Restoration activities.
- Problem Manager: Conducts RCA after service restoration.
